1923 Jayhawk

When cheerleaders urged students to drive to Lincoln for the KU-Nebraska football game on Nov. 12, 1921, and “Show ’em you’re a Jayhawk if you have to paint it on the windshield,” James O’Bryon and George Hollingbery hatched a business plan. For a fee, they painted their quirky bird on fans’ car windows. KU lost to Nebraska, 28-0, but no one blamed the new Jayhawk. In 1923, the University adopted the bird, which began to appear on jackets, sweaters and uniforms.
